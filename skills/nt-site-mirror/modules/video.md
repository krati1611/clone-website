# Video Module

Triggers: video files, provider-hosted video, CDN-hosted video, background video, scroll-linked video, embedded provider players, video masks, video posters, video-driven motion graphics.

Treat video as part of the requested baseline. Never silently substitute a static image for a video-driven source feature.

## Discovery additions

- Identify the observed video source and type: original asset, external embed, streamed provider asset, local file, generated, or protected. Record the source URL when available and mark unverified details as unknown.
- Document playback, autoplay, mute state, controls, loop, poster image, aspect ratio, crop and object-fit, masking, opacity, transforms, scroll coupling, loading behavior, provider restrictions, and responsive behavior.

## Baseline construction

### Static Mirror

Preserve authorized video files or the original provider embed where technically possible. Classify each observed video in the Asset Preservation Table and include meaningful video-driven motion in the Animation Audit.

### Editable Recreation fallback

When Static Mirror is unsuitable, recreate the observed placement and behavior needed for the requested faithful copy. Do not present a poster-only or otherwise downgraded result as equivalent video behavior; record any approved approximation as a fidelity gap.

## Validation additions

For the declared routes, viewports, and interaction states, verify that video is present, moving, positioned, masked, cropped, responsive, and behaviorally aligned with the source. If the original cannot be embedded, preserved, or recreated, report the constraint and fidelity impact. Mark blocked or unexercised states explicitly.
